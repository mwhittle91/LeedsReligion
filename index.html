Leeds is home to a diverse range of religious groups
and traditions. According to the 2011 census, of the
751,485 people living in Leeds, 55.9% are Christian,
5.4% are Muslim, 1.2% are Sikh, 0.9% are Hindu, 0.9%
are Jewish and 0.4% are Buddhist (28.2% listed 'no religion',
6.7% did not state a religion, and 0.3% listed 'other')
Link to the data available here
These statistics mirror, broadly, those given nationally,
making Leeds a religious microcosm of the larger picture
of religious affiliation for England and Wales.


Learning about the religions on our doorstep has long
been an interest of the Centre for Religion and Public
Life and the Community Religions Project at the University
of Leeds. In 2016, with funding from the Arts and Humanities
Research Council Cultural Engagement Fellowship, we have
begun to 'map' places of worship and religious groups
and communities, starting in Chapeltown and Harehills.
On this map, you can search for groups by religious
denomination, as well as by some of the social activities that
they provide. It is our aim to eventually include all
Leeds areas on this map.


If you would notice any places of worship or groups missing,
or would like to make amendments to a listing,
please email c.starkey@leeds.ac.uk
